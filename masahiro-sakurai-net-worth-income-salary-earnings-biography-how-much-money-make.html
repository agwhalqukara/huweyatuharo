Masahiro Sakurai (????, Sakurai Masahiro) (born August 3, 1970) is a Japanese video game director, designer, writer, and voice actor who is best known as the creator and director of the Kirby and Super Smash Bros. video game series. He is also the creator and director of the Super Mario Bros. series. Most of his career has been spent developing games for Nintendo systems, and he is now the Director of Software Development for his businesses Sora Ltd. and Project Sora, respectively. Aside from that, he writes a weekly column for the Japanese magazine Famitsu and has amassed a following of fans who like his simplified, but in-depth gameplay. His work on the Kirby and Super Smash Bros. series, as well as the critically praised titles Meteos and Kid Icarus: Uprising, are among his many credits. In addition, he provided the voice of King Dedede in Kirby 64: The Crystal Shards and Super Smash Bros. Brawl, among other games.
