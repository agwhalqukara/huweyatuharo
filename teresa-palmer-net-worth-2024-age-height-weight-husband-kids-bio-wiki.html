Early Life & Biography

Teresa Palmer’s birth name was Teresa Mary Palmer and was born on February 26, 1986, in the city of Adelaide, South Australia. Her parents are Paula Sanders and Kevin Palmer; her parents got divorced when she was just 3years of age. At first, she was with her mother and faced many atrocities and difficulties as her mother’s financial condition was not stable, and also her mother suffered from depression, so her childhood didn’t go very pleasing and joyful like the other children. Later her father took the responsibility of her and took her to his farm located in the Adelaide Hills.

Palmer finished her high school from Catholic Day School now called Mercedes College. Her admiration for acting was noticed over there by the school authorities as she was selected for an audition for ‘Search for a movie star’ back in 2003.

To run her living, she took odd jobs like dressing like a cartoon character in front of shops for boosting sales, she worked in fast food shops and also worked in clothing outlets.

She was an animal lover and wanted to work for the betterment of the animals. After finishing high school, she joined the university with the subjects of teaching and journalism. However, she dropped out of college as she wanted to continue a career in acting.

Personal Life

Palmer’s decision to move to Hollywood from Australia turned out to be a turning point in her career. It was not easy for her to get accustomed to a whole new different country; in the beginning, she dealt with loneliness and depression that sometimes she thought she should go back to Australia. During her initial days at Hollywood, she had been in a couple of affairs with Topher Grace and footballer Stuart Dew, but none of the relationships lasted for long.

Palmer started dating the actor cum Director Mark Webber in 2012, and they end up marrying each other at the end of the next year.

Apart from her love for acting, she is also very fond of watching football she was one of the two number one ticket holders of the Port Adelaide Football Club. Generally, it’s the tradition of the football clubs to consider a strong supporter as the number one ticket holder.
